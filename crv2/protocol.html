CONTROLLED REMOTE VIEWING

Protocol, Theory
& Basic Concepts

Theory

Controlled Remote Viewing operates on the principle that every target possesses a unique “signal line” — an informational conduit that connects the target to the viewer’s subconscious mind. The protocol systematically moves information from the subconscious into conscious awareness while rigorously suppressing analytical overlay (AOL).

The method bypasses ordinary sensory channels and relies on the viewer’s trained ability to perceive raw data fragments before the conscious mind assigns meaning.

Basic Concepts

  • 01
    Coordinate — A random 8-digit number (e.g., 4782-3910) that serves as the sole identifier of the target. The viewer remains blind to all other information.
  • 02
    Ideogram — The first instantaneous graphic response to the coordinate. A simple line drawing representing the site’s overall gestalt.
  • 03
    Signal Line — The continuous flow of information from the target. The viewer “rides” this line through each stage.
  • 04
    AOL (Analytical Overlay) — Conscious guesses or mental associations. Must be declared and set aside immediately.
  • 05
    Inclemencies — Any physical or mental distractions declared at the start of the session (fatigue, noise, etc.).

The Protocol

  1. Viewer sits at a blank sheet of paper with pen.
  2. Write the random coordinate at the top.
  3. Declare inclemencies (if any).
  4. Close eyes, relax, and receive the first ideogram.
  5. Proceed stage by stage following the exact structure taught in S1–S6.
  6. Session ends only when the viewer declares “End of session” after S6 or when data flow ceases.
  7. All work is performed blind; feedback is given only after the session is complete.

Sessions are conducted in a quiet environment. Paper and pen only. No external references allowed during viewing.
