Controlled Remote Viewing (CRV) is the original six-stage protocol developed by Ingo Swann for the U.S. intelligence community. It is a rigorous, repeatable method that trains the viewer to receive and decode information from a distant or hidden target using only a random coordinate — without any prior knowledge.
