LESSON 01 • FOUNDATION

Protocol, Theory and Basic Concepts

The complete foundation you need before touching a pen. Understand why CRV works and how to follow the exact military protocol.

1. What is Controlled Remote Viewing?

Controlled Remote Viewing (CRV) is a structured, repeatable protocol for perceiving distant or hidden targets using only the power of consciousness. It was developed by Ingo Swann in the 1970s and refined under U.S. government programs (SRI International / Stargate Project).

Key Principle: The viewer is kept completely blind to the target. A random coordinate (or encrypted ID) is provided. All data comes from subconscious perception — not imagination or guessing.

2. Scientific & Historical Theory

  • Non-local consciousness: Human awareness is not limited by space or time (supported by quantum entanglement research and decades of parapsychology data).
  • Signal-to-noise ratio: CRV trains the mind to separate genuine psychic signal from analytical overlay (AOL).
  • Stargate Project (1978–1995): Over 20 years of classified research showed statistically significant results far above chance.

3. The Official CRV Protocol

1
Preparation: Quiet room, pen & paper, comfortable chair. No distractions. State your intent aloud: “I will now perceive target [ID].”
2
Blind Target: Receive only a coordinate or ID number. You have zero front-loading.
3
Session Structure: Progress strictly through S1 → S2 → S3 → S4 → S5 → S6. Never skip stages.
4
Session Closure: Declare “End of session.” Write summary. Compare with feedback only after completion.

4. Essential Terminology

AOL – Analytical Overlay (guessing / mental noise)
Ideogram – Instant subconscious motor response (S1)
Gestalt – The overall “feel” or nature of the target
Coordinate – Random 8-digit number used as cue
Monitor – Person who oversees a session (optional for solo practice)
Feedback – Target description revealed only after session ends

Pro Tip

CRV is not “psychic guessing.” It is a trainable skill that improves dramatically with strict adherence to protocol and daily practice.
